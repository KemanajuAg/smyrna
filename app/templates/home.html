"The fates have given mankind a patient soul." — Homer, Iliad

My name is Ayhan Mese. I am a professional tourist guide based in Turkey, certified to guide in Turkish, English and Dutch. For many years I have walked the ancient ruins of Anatolia — from the marble streets of Ephesus to the walls of Troy, from the temples of Pergamon to the rock-cut tombs of Lycia — always with the same wonder and the same passion for the stories these stones hold.

Smyrna is my personal project: a multilingual blog dedicated to the ancient civilizations of Anatolia and the Greek world. The coast of western Turkey — ancient Ionia — is one of the richest archaeological landscapes on earth, yet it remains little known to the wider public. This blog is my way of sharing what I discover every day on the ground.

All articles are written with the help of historical sources, modern scholarship, and an AI assistant — making it possible to publish content in French, English, Turkish and Dutch. The name "Smyrna" refers to the ancient city now known as İzmir, traditionally one of the birthplaces of Homer, and a city that has been continuously inhabited for over 3,000 years.
